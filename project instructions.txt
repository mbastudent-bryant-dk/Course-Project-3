Course Project 3 Instructions
In this project, you will transform your previous Python-based analysis in Project One into an interactive, user-facing product using vibe coding tools.

You can choose any one of the three tasks (52 high/low trading strategy, retirement simulation, or moving average trading strategy)

Your goal is no longer just to "run code”. Instead, you will build a simple application that other people can access and explore.

Objective
You will take one of your previous mini projects and convert it into a fully interactive web-based tool where users can:

Input assumptions
Experiment with different scenarios
Instantly see results and insights
Think of this as turning your assignment into a real financial decision tool or simulation app.

Core Requirements
Your app must include the following:

1. User Interaction (Required)
Users must be able to change key inputs.

Examples:

Retirement project: contribution amount, return assumption, retirement age
Trading project: moving average windows, holding period, time range
2. Real-Time Output
Your app should immediately update results when inputs change.

Examples:

Final wealth or probability of retirement success
Trading returns, number of trades, risk metrics
3. Visualization (Strongly Recommended)
Use charts to help users understand results.

Examples:

Wealth over time
Buy/sell signals on price charts
Distribution of outcomes (for simulations)
4. Decision Insight (Very Important)
Your app should help users answer a question, not just display numbers.

Examples:

“Will I reach my retirement goal?”
“Which trading strategy performs better?”
“How sensitive are results to my assumptions?”
5. Clean and Simple Interface
Clear labels
Easy-to-understand inputs
Organized layout
No unnecessary complexity
Vibe Coding Tools
You may use tools such as:

Claude Code
Google AI Studio
Or other vibe coding tools
What to Submit
The Design Document (1–2 paragraphs) explaining:
What your app does
What inputs you 
What decision/problem it helps solve
The structure of the app
A short video of demostraction (5 - 10 minutes)
Live App Link (or runnable app)
Evaluation Criteria
Your project will be evaluated based on:

The Design Document (the most improtant)
The App
Functionality (Does it work correctly?)
Interactivity (Can users explore different scenarios?)
Clarity (Is it easy to understand?)
Usefulness (Does it provide meaningful insight?)
Important Note
This is not a front-end design competition.
Focus on:

making your model usable
helping users learn something
translating financial logic into decisions
